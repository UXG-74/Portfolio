OPENING (intro under meta - two paragraphs)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Stakeholders weren't asking for a cosmetic pass; they wanted sharper
differentiation and an experience that lasted after novelty faded. I owned the
UX thread: research into product reality, interaction patterns, and a tone and
visual language that belonged to Prevayl - not default health-tech chrome.

Discovery ran in focused bursts with early technical reality checks. Custom GPTs
compressed interview notes into themes; Pencil and Figma supported fast visual
passes; Cursor with Claude Code let us pressure-test flows and copy.
Decisions stayed anchored in usability, feasibility, and clarity.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NARRATIVE BLOCK (three paragraphs)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Companion apps die when hierarchy stops at dashboards. Empty states, edge
cases, and failure paths needed the same polish as marketing screens - every
surface had to answer what matters now.

The work assumed uneven light, impatient thumbs, and half-known concepts:
calm rhythm, predictable components, and language that rewarded a glance rather
than demanding a manual.

Outcome: clearer comprehension in testing, a tighter frame around core
journeys, and a handover toolkit engineering could implement - grounded in
critique, not aspiration slides.

SECTIONS

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
One product story from marketing through logged-in ritual.

Body
The audience spans committed athletes and people who want steadier training
signals - premium without austerity, explanatory without condescension.

Brand, UX, and copy moved together. If a screen failed the skim test for
“what’s next?”, it went back for another pass.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE CHALLENGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
Sequencing trust - not shipping another chart.

Body
People need to know what they're wearing, what's measured, and what to do with
an insight - in that order, without being talked down to. Depth was
choreographed: orientation first, jargon on demand.

Clear IA, a tight component set, and narrative glue between sessions, trends,
and recovery. Visual polish served legibility - not decoration alone.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DISCOVERY / TRUST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
Credibility before specification.

Body
High-consideration hardware starts with curiosity, then proof. Typography,
spacing, and terminology had to build confidence before dense data.

Atmosphere was deliverable: calm, intentional, no performative novelty - with
structure underneath for scan, compare, and return visits.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REQUIREMENTS (colour / accessibility)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
Contrast as a baseline, not garnish.

Body
Changing light and hurry change how interfaces are judged. State design and
focus order were product requirements - deep anchors and breathable surfaces
kept signal in front.

Colour mapped to intent: quieter story zones; sharper contrast for action and anomalies.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FRAMEWORK (components / typography)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
Components that scale without drifting.

Body
Worked toward a coherent kit - cards, modules, wayfinding - across onboarding,
marketing, and the logged-in shell.

Typography split roles: serif warmth for brand moments; neutral sans wherever
density and numerals dominate.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE LAUNCH / HANDOVER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
Built for real routines - plus what happens when tech misbehaves.

Body
First-week onboarding, return-after-break, Bluetooth hiccups - same rigour as
hero states. Reassurance where anxiety spikes, not buried footnotes.

Handover documented rationale and boundaries so intent survived implementation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OUTCOMES / COLLABORATION (split across page if needed)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
What shifted; how we worked together.

Body
Stakeholders aligned on one value story; testing showed quicker comprehension on
core paths. Engineering and product shared a clearer picture of what shipped next.

Partnership stayed in critiques and specs - not late redlines - with decisions
written down so builders didn't guess intent.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Headline
What stayed with me.

Body
Wearable UX is translation: signal → meaning → something someone can act on
tomorrow morning. Tools widen exploration; quality still lives in iteration,
trade-offs, and candour within the team.

Detail, motion, and the longer narrative: Framer link above.
